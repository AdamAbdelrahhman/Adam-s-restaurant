Heritage

A timeline of firsts.

In 1886, Karl Benz patented the first practical automobile — a three-wheeled vehicle powered by a single-cylinder engine. What began as a mechanical curiosity became the blueprint for an entire industry.

Every model since carries that same restless standard: engineer first, decorate never. The three-pointed star was never just a badge — it was a promise that the next mile would be safer, faster, and quieter than the last.
